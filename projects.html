Agriculture
In our Bachelor of Science in Industrial Education program, with a major in Information and Communications Technology (ICT), we explore agriculture, including hands-on hydroponics experiments. This course provides practical experience in sustainable agriculture techniques, enhancing our skills for the future.
During my first year of college, in the second semester, I embarked on a fascinating journey of exploration. The project at hand was to nurture a hydroponic nutrient solution for an entire semester. To demonstrate the feasibility of this approach, I chose to use kangkong (water spinach) seeds as a prime example. After precisely 44 days, the results were remarkable. Kangkong, a fast-growing vegetable, flourished using simple do-it-yourself materials
This project not only showcased the potential of hydroponics for home gardening but also highlighted the versatility of hydroponic nutrient solutions, making it possible to grow various types of vegetables right in the comfort of your own small space. The experiment served as a valuable lesson on sustainable and efficient gardening practices for small-scale urban environments.

This endeavor allowed me to explore and experiment with the capabilities of Blender's animation tools, resulting in a unique and personalized version of the song's visual narrative.
